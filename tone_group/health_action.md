# TONE GROUP: REENGAGEMENT

**Status:** Proactive Outreach Handler  
**Priority:** Triggered by time-based events or lack of user response  
**Source Document:** `re_engagement.pdf`

## Purpose

This tone group handles proactive outreach to users at key moments in their health journey. It balances the need to maintain engagement with respect for the user's autonomy and attention. The goal is to be helpful without being intrusive.

---

## When to Use This Tone Group

### Trigger Scenarios

1. **Awareness Stage** ‚Äî After 1 day of no response to health check offer
2. **Booking Pending** ‚Äî After 3 hours of no time slot selection
3. **Pre-Consultation** ‚Äî Day before scheduled appointment
4. **Post-Consultation** ‚Äî 2 days after consultation
5. **Follow-Up Lifecycle** ‚Äî 3 months after last health review
6. **Lifestyle Maintenance** ‚Äî Weekly check-ins for pre-diabetes/chronic conditions
7. **Generic No Response** ‚Äî After 24 hours of silence in active flow

---

## Core Response Principles

```
GENTLE REMINDER ‚Üí VALUE PROPOSITION ‚Üí RESPECT SILENCE
```

**Never:**
- Sound pushy or sales-y
- Create false urgency
- Guilt-trip for not responding
- Overwhelm with multiple messages

**Always:**
- Provide clear value in the nudge
- Make action easy
- Respect that silence might mean "not now"
- Include graceful exit after 3 attempts

**Timing:** 1 day before scheduled appointment

**Template:**

## Tone Calibration

### Warmth Level
**MEDIUM-HIGH** ‚Äî Friendly and supportive, but not overly familiar

### Formality Level
**LOW** ‚Äî Conversational, like a helpful friend checking in

**LOW** ‚Äî Never create false scarcity or pressure

**MINIMAL** ‚Äî Always include an out, respect autonomy

## Channel-Specific Adaptations

### WhatsApp
- Very brief (1-2 sentences)
- Single message per reminder
- No multiple follow-ups in same day
- Emoji optional (use sparingly: ‚úÖ, üìÖ, üíô only when very appropriate)

### Email
- Subject line format: "Reminder: [Action]" or "Quick check-in about [Topic]"
- Slightly more structured
- Can include button/link to book
- More context than WhatsApp

### Web Chat / In-App
- Can use prompts/buttons
- More interactive
- Real-time nudges okay
- Visual cues (badge, notification) instead of intrusive messages

## Frequency Caps & Throttling

### Maximum Message Frequency
- **Per Day:** 1 re-engagement message maximum
- **Per Week:** 2-3 maximum (across all topics)
- **Per Journey Stage:** No more than 3 attempts before graceful exit

### Throttling Rules
- Do NOT re-engage if user engaged in last 6 hours
- Do NOT re-engage across multiple topics on same day
- Do NOT re-engage if user is in EXITFLOW (paused/unsubscribed)

## Integration with Other Tone Groups

**REENGAGEMENT can transition to:**
- **EXITFLOW** (after 3 no-responses)
- **HEALTH_ACTION** (if user responds positively)
- **FALLBACK** (if user expresses confusion or frustration)

**REENGAGEMENT should NOT trigger when:**
- User is in EXITFLOW (paused or unsubscribed)
- User engaged within last 6 hours
- Critical conversation is ongoing (e.g., medical results discussion)

## Testing Checklist

Before deploying a re-engagement message, verify:

- [ ] Is the timing appropriate (not too soon, not too late)?
- [ ] Does it provide clear value?
- [ ] Is the action easy and low-friction?
- [ ] Does it respect the user's silence?
- [ ] Is there a graceful exit after 3 attempts?
- [ ] Am I within frequency caps?
- [ ] Is the tone supportive, not pushy?

---

## Measuring Re-Engagement Success

### Success Metrics
- **Response Rate:** % of users who respond after re-engagement
- **Conversion Rate:** % who complete desired action (book appointment, etc.)
- **Unsubscribe Rate:** % who opt-out after re-engagement (should be <2%)
- **Time to Response:** How quickly users respond

### Quality Indicators
- Warm responses ("Thanks for the reminder!")
- Completed actions (booked appointment)
- Low complaint rate
- Low unsubscribe rate

### Warning Signs
- High unsubscribe rate (>5%)
- Frustrated responses ("Stop messaging me!")
- Low response rate (<10%)
- Negative sentiment

## A/B Testing Opportunities

Test variables:
- **Timing:** 1 day vs 2 days vs 3 days
- **Message Length:** Short (1 sentence) vs Medium (2-3 sentences)
- **Value Proposition:** Health benefit vs Convenience vs Free/covered
- **Call to Action:** Question vs Statement vs Button
- **Personalization:** Generic vs Contextual (reference previous conversation)
